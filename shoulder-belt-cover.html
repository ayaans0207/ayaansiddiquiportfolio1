Project Biography

This project involved designing shoulder belt cover mounts for the Formula SAE race vehicle to improve harness routing and protect the restraint system from nearby components. Because the design area was tightly constrained, careful attention had to be given to geometry, clearances, and installation feasibility.

My role involved CAD development and design refinement with a focus on packaging, function, and compliance. The mount had to achieve its protective purpose without introducing unnecessary complexity or interference with other systems in the vehicle.

Working on this project strengthened my experience in detailed chassis design and reinforced how important small design decisions are in motorsport engineering, especially when multiple subsystems compete for limited space.
