Project Biography

This project involved designing seatbelt inserts for the UCalgary Formula SAE vehicle to improve restraint positioning and support safe harness routing within the chassis. The inserts needed to be secure, manufacturable, and compatible with the surrounding structure.

I worked through the design process from CAD development to fabrication-focused consideration, ensuring the parts could be produced efficiently while still meeting functional requirements. Fitment and real chassis integration were important parts of validating the design.

The project was especially valuable in showing how small components can still have a major effect on safety, usability, and assembly quality. It strengthened my experience in detailed design work, fabrication-aware decision making, and engineering for real vehicle applications.
